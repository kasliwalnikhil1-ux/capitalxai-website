Finding the right investor isn't just about finding *any* investor — it's about finding the one whose thesis, stage preference, and sector focus align perfectly with your startup. AI-powered investor discovery makes this possible at a scale that manual research simply can't match.

## What Is AI-Powered Investor Discovery?

At its core, AI-powered investor discovery uses machine learning algorithms to analyze vast datasets of investor activity, preferences, and portfolio patterns. It then matches these profiles against your startup's characteristics to surface the most relevant opportunities.

Think of it as a recommendation engine — similar to how Netflix suggests shows based on your viewing history, but for matching startups with investors.

## The Data Behind the Matching

The quality of any AI system depends on its data. Modern investor discovery platforms aggregate information from multiple sources:

### Public Data Sources
- **SEC filings** — Fund sizes, LP compositions, and investment mandates
- **Press releases** — Announced deals and fund closings
- **LinkedIn activity** — Posts, comments, and connection patterns
- **Conference appearances** — Speaking engagements and panel topics

### Proprietary Signals
- **Email engagement patterns** — How investors respond to different types of outreach
- **Portfolio performance data** — Which sectors are generating returns
- **Fund lifecycle indicators** — Whether a fund is actively deploying or in harvest mode
- **Co-investment networks** — Which investors frequently syndicate together

## How the Matching Algorithm Works

The matching process typically involves several layers:

### Layer 1: Hard Filters
First, the system eliminates obvious mismatches:
- Stage mismatch (e.g., Series B fund for a pre-seed startup)
- Geographic restrictions
- Sector exclusions
- Minimum/maximum check sizes

### Layer 2: Relevance Scoring
Next, each remaining investor gets a relevance score based on:
- **Thesis alignment** — How well your sector matches their stated focus
- **Recency of activity** — Investors who deployed recently are more likely to be active
- **Portfolio complementarity** — Whether your startup fills a gap in their portfolio
- **Success patterns** — Investors who've had exits in your space

### Layer 3: Timing Signals
Finally, the system considers timing:
- Fund age and remaining capital
- Recent hiring activity (new associates = active sourcing)
- Public statements about investment pace
- Seasonal patterns in deal-making
